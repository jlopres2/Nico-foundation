The Nico Foundation



Hello, we are Meg and Salvatore Lopresti. We grew up in Franklinville, NJ, and were Delsea High School sweethearts. We were fortunate enough to have three beautiful children. Our third child was extremely unexpected, but he was also a gift from God and just what our family needed. We decided to move to a bigger house to accommodate our growing family and find our forever home for them. A couple of weeks after moving in, our absolute worst nightmare happened... our youngest son, Nico, passed away unexpectedly. The pain is inexplicable; there are no words. Even writing this feels surreal. Nico had just turned two, three weeks prior.

He was the most beautiful, spunky, giggly, mischievous, smiley, rough (Bam Bam as we would call him), loving mama’s boy you could meet. No words can express who he was and how much he means to us. Any parent will understand the love you have for your child surpasses any emotion, bond, feelings of love you could ever explain. During our loss, which now feels more like a blur and an unexplainable time, we were extremely fortunate to have a village of love and support. Family and friends traveled states over, slept in our home, brought us food, helped with our other two children, and something that is farthest from your imaginable thoughts financially assisted us for the unexpected funeral costs, luncheon, and memorial item costs. Nico’s passing was accidental drowning. The PTSD and trauma we have endured are unexplainable. The costs that come with that are just as unexpected. In reflection, we cannot imagine what it would be to go through this again without the village and financial help we received.

We do not want Nico’s life to be in vain; we wanted him to be remembered and to bring happiness to others as he brought to us. We always said he was going to be a slugger because he could throw a ball harder than his older siblings and loved to play catch (when he wasn’t tackling his brother and sister to the ground). Sunday softball games are a regular way of life for our family; Nico loved watching his dad play and run around the fields. His team, TMT, would go on to win a championship following his death; the family bond we have created with the softball community has been a large part of our healing. We wanted to create a softball tournament in honor of Nico, a day to have fun, be with friends and family, cheer each other on, have laughing children running around cheering on their dad or mom, and creating core memories for years to come.

This tournament's donations will go towards South Jersey funeral costs for children. We wanted to create this day of love to pass on to the families enduring these horrific realities and make just one less thing for them to handle when they should be healing and facing more important issues. We are strong supporters of our first responders and want to make them a part of this day, as they were there for us during the toughest moments, and they also deal with difficult situations on a daily basis. They mean so much to our community, and we could not thank them enough. This will be the first fundraiser of many. We hope to create an atmosphere where everyone looks forward to the event every year, to infinity and beyond for our little boy and the families that will come after us.
